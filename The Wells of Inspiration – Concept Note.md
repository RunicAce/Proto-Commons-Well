# The Wells of Inspiration – Concept Note

**Author:** Anonymous  
**Date:** 2026-01-03  
**License:** Public Domain – ideas may be freely read, shared, and adapted

---

## 1. Motivation

In current innovation systems, many good ideas fail to reach those who can implement them.  

- Individuals often have valuable speculative ideas but lack the means to execute them.  
- Capitalism ties idea existence to execution: if you cannot implement, your idea often dies.  
- Social platforms are ephemeral or require participation; ideas may vanish even if good.

The Wells of Inspiration aim to solve this structural gap.

---

## 2. Concept Overview

The Wells are **digital repositories of human speculation**, designed to:

- Capture ideas too early, wild, or incomplete to implement immediately.  
- Make them publicly accessible for those who can turn them into actionable projects.  
- Separate **idea contribution** from **implementation effort**, allowing ideas to survive even if the originator cannot execute them.

---

## 3. Structure: Two Complementary Wells

### A. Commons Well (Open Source / Public Domain)

- Submissions are fully anonymized.  
- Ideas are released into the **public domain**.  
- No attribution is guaranteed; credit is intentionally removed.  
- Focus: **maximum freedom for speculation**.  
- Audience: anyone seeking inspiration, from scientists to educators to artists.  

**Disclaimer for contributors:**  
> "Ideas submitted to the Commons Well are released to the public domain and anonymized. If you wish to maintain attribution, please submit to the Attributed Well."

---

### B. Attributed Well (Commercial / Credit-Preserving)

- Contributors may optionally include identity or pseudonym.  
- Inspiration is formally acknowledged.  
- Implementers may claim legal ownership of the resulting work, while citing the originator as inspiration.  
- Focus: **safe integration into commercial or institutional innovation pipelines**.  
- Audience: corporations, startups, governments, and research institutions.

---

## 4. Process and Use

1. **Submission**: Anyone may submit an idea to either Well.  
2. **Categorization**: Ideas are tagged by domain (e.g., energy, education, governance, arts).  
3. **Browsing and Inspiration**: Implementers browse Wells for ideas to refine or make plausible.  
4. **Attribution / Implementation**:  
   - Commons Well: ideas are free to adapt, no attribution required.  
   - Attributed Well: implementers credit originators as inspiration, ownership applies to execution.

---

## 5. Core Principles

- **Speculation ≠ Ownership**: Ideation is free and non-rival; execution is scarce and rivalrous.  
- **Collective Momentum**: Ideas thrive in ecosystems, not in private isolation.  
- **Freedom and Safety**: Contributors can share without fear of exploitation or disputes.  
- **Durability over Sociality**: Ideas persist even for contributors who do not maintain social accounts or interact.

---

## 6. Philosophical Note

The Wells are designed for **impact, not recognition**.  
- Contributors may never implement or see their ideas realized.  
- The primary goal is to **place ideas in a persistent, public space** where they can influence others.  
- Not everyone wants to be a hero; the Wells allow contributors to support heroes quietly.

---

## 7. Intended Outcome

- Reduce idea loss due to structural constraints of current capitalism and social systems.  
- Provide scientists, innovators, and creators with a **persistent, global pool of speculation**.  
- Encourage **cross-disciplinary collaboration**, even if contributors remain anonymous.  
- Create a **mechanism for separating ideation from execution**, allowing ideas to flourish without forcing originators to build them.
